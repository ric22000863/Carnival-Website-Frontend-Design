About

The Bridgwater Carnival

The annual Bridgwater Carnival is a vibrant parade of illuminated carts that attracts over 150,000 visitors each year. With roots dating back to the Gunpowder Plot in 1605, the carnival has been a Bridgwater tradition for over 400 years. The streets of Bridgwater come alive with the sound of music on Carnival Day, beginning at 10:30am. The main carnival procession begins at 6:00pm.


Squibbing

Squibbing is a unique tradition to Bridgwater, where "squibbers" light a large firework called a "squib" above their head. The effect is bright light being fired into the air along the road in one great long trail of fire.

Support the Carnival

Bridgwater Carnival is very expensive to stage so to secure its future we have to raise considerable amounts of money. Donations and sponsorships are very valuable to us.
